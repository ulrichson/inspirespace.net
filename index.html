Welcome to Inspirespace.

With this app you can craft beautiful moodboards and freely zoom in and out on the canvas. Your moodboard is stored on your computer - no login required!

  • You can drag & drop images onto the canvas or use the Upload button to add images.
  • Select one or more images by making a selection on the canvas and use the handles to transform images.
  • To delete selected images simply press Delete or Backspace on your keyboard.
  • You can pan the canvas by holding Space + move with the mouse. You zoom in and out by holding Space + scroll with the mouse.

Background

Thanks for using Inspirespace! It's a personal project that evolved into this app. My name is Ulrich Lehner and I'm a designer and developer based in Vienna. If you want to know more about me please visit my online portfolio or just have a look at my photographs. I'm happy to share my thoughts with you why I created this app.

Moodboards are physical collection of pictures as means of inspiration to convey a certain direction for design-driven work. It's used by visual designers to arrange inspiring user interfaces on a canvas and explore the look and feel of an app.

Beside the analog moodboards there are existing online solutions which have a similar objective to collect pictures. However, current digital moodboards are limited in the free arrangement of pictures. I couldn't find any suitable tool that will break this barrier and therefore I started to build it on my own. Over time I created Inspirespace, which bridges this gap and allows the users to arrange images freely on a canvas. The canvas itself can be zoomed seamlessly to refine and explore areas of interests. This is another dimension that cannot be achieved by the analog counterparts. You will have a continuous overview and never lose orientation even when many pictures are present.

In the future I plan to enhance Inspirespace to bring it to the next level:

  • Utilize object recognition to automatically tag images with the power of client-side machine learning
  • Allow text-based search
  • Support for other media types, e.g. text or video
  • Optimize it for mobile usage, e.g. touch support, progressive web apps etc.
  • Allow cross-browser syncing
  • Enhance for other use cases, such as a collaboration tool during meetings

Your images are stored in your browser (using PouchDB) and are not transferred to a central storage. This also means that you can only use the app with one browser. In the future I consider to optionally sync the data to a storage provider, such as Dropbox, where only you have access to.

Do you have feedback? For now please open an issue on GitHub or contact me via my website.
